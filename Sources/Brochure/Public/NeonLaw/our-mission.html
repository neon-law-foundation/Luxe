Our Mission

We believe that every person on the planet has the right to their own data, the right to be forgotten, and the right to have their problems heard fairly in our justice system.

Protecting Your Fundamental Rights

At Neon Law Foundation, our mission is simple yet profound: to protect and advocate for the fundamental privacy rights that every individual deserves. We operate on three core principles that guide everything we do:

Right to Your Own Data

You should have complete control over what information about you is collected, how it's used, who has access to it, and for how long it's retained. Your personal data belongs to you, not to corporations or governments.

Right to be Forgotten

You should have the ability to have your personal information deleted and removed from digital platforms when you choose. The internet should not be a permanent record that haunts you forever.

Right to Fair Justice

Your problems and concerns should be heard fairly in our justice system, regardless of your background, circumstances, or technical knowledge. Justice should be accessible to everyone.

Our Approach

We believe that protecting privacy rights requires a multi-faceted approach that combines legal expertise, technical knowledge, and community engagement. Our work is guided by principles of accessibility, education, and empowerment.

Education First

We believe that knowledge is the first line of defense. Through comprehensive educational programs, we empower individuals with the information they need to protect their privacy and security.

Accessible Support

Our services are designed to be accessible to everyone, regardless of technical expertise or financial resources. Privacy protection should not be a privilege for the few.

Community Focused

We work closely with communities to understand their unique needs and challenges, tailoring our programs and services to provide the most effective support.

Advocacy and Action

Beyond education, we actively advocate for policy changes and legal protections that strengthen privacy rights and digital security for all individuals.

Fellowship Program

Our fellowship program provides opportunities for law students and recent graduates to gain practical experience in privacy law, digital rights advocacy, and civil rights protection. Fellows work directly with our team on real cases and initiatives while developing expertise in this rapidly evolving field.

What Fellows Gain

  • Hands-on experience with privacy law and digital rights cases
  • Mentorship from experienced privacy and civil rights attorneys
  • Opportunity to work on cutting-edge legal and policy issues
  • Professional development and networking opportunities
  • Experience with community education and outreach programs
  • Contribution to meaningful social justice initiatives

Fellowship Requirements

  • Current law student (2L or 3L) or recent law school graduate
  • Strong interest in privacy law, digital rights, or civil rights
  • Commitment to social justice and public service
  • Excellent research and writing skills
  • Ability to work independently and as part of a team
  • Interest in community education and outreach

Apply for Fellowship

Join our team and make a meaningful impact on privacy rights and digital security protection. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis.

Application Process:

  • Submit resume and cover letter
  • Provide academic transcripts
  • Include writing sample
  • Two professional references
